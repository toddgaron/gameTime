The hypothetical BoardGameGeek score is found using a cross validated boosted decision tree. Boosting takes a series of weak learners and glues them together to create a stronger model. We use the algorithm AdaBoost because it is relatively quick to train and only keeps the most important features.

Once we have a games score we calculate the similarity of that game with every other game in the dataset and output those games and then the games most similar to those games as you'll see on the next page.
